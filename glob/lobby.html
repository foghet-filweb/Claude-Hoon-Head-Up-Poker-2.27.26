Head-Up Poker — How to Play

No house.
No rake.
No server.
No eye in the sky.
Unbreakable cryptography.

Gaming is peer-to-peer. Rooms are 1:1 play. Stakes are Fhloston coins — play money issued freely to every player. There is no real-money risk.
Fair Gaming
Cards are shuffled using SRA — a commutative encryption protocol built on a 2,048-bit prime modulus drawn from RFC 3526 Group 14, the same standard used by modern TLS, providing 112 bits of effective security. Neither player ever sees the other's hole cards until showdown, and neither player — nor the server — can predict or manipulate the order of the deck. Each card is encrypted independently by both players and revealed only when dealt, with no trusted third party required. All communications are authenticated via Urbit's Ames protocol using Curve25519 elliptic curve cryptography at 128 bits of security — estimated cracking times exceed the age of the universe. Cheating is not a matter of trust. It is a matter of mathematics. It is impossible.
How to Find a Game
Browse the lobby chat to get a feel for who's around. Use the Reputation lookup to check a ship's history before you commit. Enter their ship name in the Challenge field, set your stakes, and send the challenge.
Stakes
You set five parameters before a challenge: small blind, big blind, minimum raise, and at least one session limit — a number of hands, a total chip cap, or both. These cannot be altered once a challenge is accepted. The room dissolves automatically when the first limit is reached. Fhloston coins are issued freely upon entry and are valid here only.

The small blind and big blind are mandatory opening bets posted before cards are dealt. The big blind is typically twice the small blind. Blinds ensure there is always a worthy pot, and prevent players from continuously folding.
Accepting a Challenge
When a challenge arrives you'll hear a soft tone and see a notification. You have sixty seconds to accept or decline. A comment field allows those who decline to counter with more acceptable terms.
At the Table
Standard Texas Hold'em rules apply. Two hole cards, five community cards, best five-card hand wins. The challenged party posts the big blind. The challenger is the dealer and posts the small blind, acting first before the flop. The button alternates each hand thereafter.
Reputation
Every completed hand updates both players' reputation scores. Complaints can be filed for abusive behavior. Your reputation is visible to everyone in the lobby and travels with you while on Fhloston.
Built to Grow
Head-Up Poker architecture supports signed hand histories, cryptographic dispute resolution, and a complete blockchain audit trail for every hand ever played. Bridging to existing chains for real-money escrow and wagering are legal decisions, not engineering ones.
